<h4 id="screenshot-setup-tutorial">Screenshot Setup Tutorial:</h4>
<div class="level1">
<ol>
 	<li class="vspace">When you open the WordPress app, tap on <strong>Add Self-Hosted Site</strong>:
<img class="shadow wp-image-303 aligncenter" src="https://stateu.org/docs/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/2019104.1-smartphone-app.gif" alt="log into your WordPress site from your smartphone" width="320" height="568" /></li>
 	<li class="vspace">Then, you will be able to enter your WordPress site credentials:
These credentials come from your Installatron page of WordPress. To access these credentials, first find the applications you added with Installatron by clicking on the <strong>My Apps</strong> icon.<img class="shadow alignnone wp-image-305 size-full" src="https://stateu.org/docs/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/2019104.1-installatron-my-apps.gif" alt="My apps icon" width="840" height="187" /></li>
 	<li class="vspace">Next, click the title of your installed instance of WordPress:
<img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-161" src="https://stateu.org/docs/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/2018-04-02-at-10.49-AM.png" alt="" width="1437" height="530" /></li>
 	<li class="vspace">From here, under the <strong>overview</strong> tab, you can access your WordPress site credentials. You may change your user password by filling in the field next to <strong>password</strong>, scrolling down, and clicking the <strong>Save all</strong> button. Note that this sometimes takes a few minutes, so even if it doesn't look like anything is happening, do not refresh your page after clicking save.
<img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-162" src="https://stateu.org/docs/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/2018-04-02-at-10.54-AM.png" alt="" width="1440" height="633" /></li>
 	<li class="vspace">With these credentials, enter them into the WordPress App along with the url for your WordPress website and select <strong>Next.</strong></li>
 	<li class="vspace">On the next page, you will see all of the WordPress websites you have added to the WordPress App. Continue to the site you just added.</li>
 	<li class="vspace">To start a new post, tap on the <strong>Pencil Icon</strong>:
<img class="shadow alignnone wp-image-299" src="https://stateu.org/docs/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/2019104.1-app5.gif" alt="pencil icon lets your create/edit blog post" width="320" height="576" /></li>
 	<li class="vspace">On this page, add your <strong>Title</strong> and <strong>Content. </strong>You can edit the properties of text by selecting the text and the different <strong>Text Property Buttons</strong>:
<img class="shadow alignnone wp-image-309" src="https://stateu.org/docs/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/2019104.1-smartphone-format-options.gif" alt="Screen shot of Text Property options" width="320" height="568" /></li>
 	<li class="vspace">To view the progress of your post, select "<strong>..."</strong> on the top right of the screen and select <strong>Preview</strong>:
<img class="shadow alignnone wp-image-311" src="https://stateu.org/docs/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/2019104.1-app7.gif" alt="" width="320" height="568" /></li>
 	<li class="vspace">When finished, select <strong>Publish</strong>:
<img class="shadow alignnone wp-image-296" src="https://stateu.org/docs/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/2019104.1-app8.gif" alt="Publish post" width="320" height="568" /></li>
</ol>
</div>
<div class="level4">

Now when you visit your WordPress webpage, you will see your new blog post!
